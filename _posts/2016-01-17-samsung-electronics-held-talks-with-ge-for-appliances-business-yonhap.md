<span class="focusParagraph" readability="5"><p><span class="articleLocation">SEOUL</span> Tech giant Samsung Electronics Co Ltd held talks with General Electric Co about buying the U.S. company's appliances businesses but negotiations broke down on antitrust issues, South Korea's Yonhap News agency reported on Saturday. </p></span><span id="midArticle_0"/><p>The report said Samsung initiated the talks after General Electric (GE) walked away from a deal with Electrolux AB due to opposition from U.S. antitrust regulators.</p><span id="midArticle_1"/><p>Without citing its sources, Yonhap said GE ended talks with Samsung after U.S. regulators expressed concerns about antitrust issues. The South Korean firm is a major player in the U.S. appliances market.</p><span id="midArticle_2"/>
        
        <span class="first-article-divide"/><p>China's Haier Group said it would buy the GE business for $5.4 billion.</p><span id="midArticle_3"/>
        
        <span class="second-article-divide"/><p>A Samsung spokeswoman declined to comment, while GE could not be immediately reached for comment.   </p><span id="midArticle_4"/>
